The Library Analogy

📚

You're in a library looking for books about "cooking pasta".

Q (Query): Your search slip — "I want books about cooking pasta."

K (Key): The index cards on every book — "I'm about Italian cuisine."

V (Value): The actual book content — all 300 pages of recipes.

O (Output): The summary you write after reading the matching books.

Q×K finds the books. (Your search × index cards = which books match.)

Softmax ranks them. (Some books are more relevant than others.)

×V reads them. (Take the best matching books and read their content.)

O summarizes. (Combine everything you learned into one answer.)

Why LoRA Targets Q and V (and Not K)

ModuleWhat It DoesLoRA?Reason
Q (query)"What should this word look for?"✅ YesControls WHERE attention goes. High impact on behavior.
V (value)"What content does this word carry?"✅ YesControls WHAT information is passed. High impact on outputs.
K (key)"How can other words find me?"❌ Usually notQ and V together already capture the changes. Adding K doubles adapter size for small gain.
O (output)"Combine all attention heads"⚠️ OptionalImproves quality but doubles adapter size. Add for better results if you have the memory.
🧠 Why Q+V is enough: Think of adapting the model like training a new employee. You need to change WHAT they look for (Q) and WHAT they say (V). HOW others find them (K) usually doesn't need to change — the existing keys already work fine for search. You're changing the questions asked and the answers given, not the index system.
