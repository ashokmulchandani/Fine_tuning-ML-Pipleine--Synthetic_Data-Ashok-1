Estimating Learning Rate & Steps

How many steps will it take to converge? Pick lr or steps — the math estimates the other.

🧠 The Key Relationship (Exponential Decay)

Each step, the gap shrinks by roughly the same factor:
  gap_next ≈ gap_current × |1 − 2 × lr|

Number of steps to reach target gap:
  N ≈ log(G_target / G_start) / log(|1 − 2×lr|)

Learning rate for desired number of steps:
  lr ≈ (1 − (G_target/G_start)^(1/N)) / 2

This ignores the small task-gradient term. For precise results, simulation is better — but this gets you within ~10%.

💡 Rules of Thumb for W1 (gap = 0.10 → 0.01, equilibrium at ~0.0074):
  • lr = 0.05 → ~30 steps (fast, may overshoot equilibrium)
  • lr = 0.02 → ~70 steps (balanced — our animation)
  • lr = 0.01 → ~150 steps (smooth, safe)
  • lr = 0.001 → ~1300 steps (painfully slow)
  • lr > 0.25 → Oscillates. Weight bounces past bucket each step.
  • The limit: lr must be < 0.5 for the estimate to predict convergence (|1−2×lr| < 1)

⚠️ Why target 0.01 and not 0.0003? The task term (0.16×(W−W₀)) creates an equilibrium at gap ≈ 0.0074. The weight can never permanently go below this — like a spring that gets harder to stretch. So "converged" means reaching the equilibrium zone, not absolute zero.
